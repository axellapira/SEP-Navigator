About SEP Navigator

What is this?

SEP Navigator is a visual map of the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy. It lets you explore ~1,800 entries by their philosophical hierarchy and by who cites whom.

Two views

Taxonomy — a treemap of philosophy's branches: History, Moral, Metaphysics, Knowledge, Logic. Drill in, drill back out.

Citations — a network graph where each node is an article and each line a citation. Bigger nodes are more cited.

Tips

Drag windows by their title bar. Resize from the bottom-right. Hit the green light to maximize. Hit yellow to send a window to the dock.

How to use

Taxonomy view

Click any cell to dive in. Hit Back to step out, Reset to return to the top. Article cells open the actual SEP page.

Citations view

Filter by Top Category and Subcategory. Use the search box to jump to any article. The 10 largest nodes are always labelled.

Window controls

Three buttons top-left of each window: close, minimize to dock, maximize. Drag the bottom-right corner to resize. Drag the title bar to move.

Dock

Anything minimized or closed lands in the dock at the bottom. Click to bring it back.
